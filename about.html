Why Varna

Varna, often referred to as the "Sea Capital of Bulgaria," is a vibrant city located on the Black Sea coast. It offers a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Explore the Archaeological Museum

The Archaeological Museum of Varna is a treasure trove of artifacts that provide insights into the city's rich history. From ancient pottery to stunning gold artifacts, visitors can journey through millennia of civilization.

Relax on Beautiful Beaches

Varna boasts some of the most picturesque beaches along the Black Sea coast. Whether you're seeking relaxation or adventure, the sandy shores and crystal-clear waters offer the perfect setting for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.

Indulge in Local Cuisine

Experience the flavors of Bulgaria by sampling the local cuisine in Varna. From traditional dishes like banitsa and shopska salad to fresh seafood delicacies, there's something to satisfy every palate.

Discover the Cathedral of the Assumption of the Virgin. This is the largest and most impressive Orthodox church in Varna, and one of the symbols of the city. It was built in the late 19th century, in a neo-Byzantine style, with a domed roof and a bell tower. The interior is decorated with frescoes, icons, and stained glass windows. You can also climb up the tower and admire the panoramic view of the city.
